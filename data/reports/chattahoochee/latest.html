Chattahoochee River 48-Hour Fishing Outlook

Quick Read

Rating: Fair to good (6.0/10).

The Chattahoochee River fishing outlook is fair to good with warming water and recently stocked trout. Expect active nymph fishing with focus on oxygenated runs and structure.

Best Window

Best window: day1 morning.

Day 1 morning is best due to cooler temperatures and low wind, allowing trout to feed actively in riffles and pocket water, matching well with early and late window tactics.

Current River Conditions

USGS reports water temperature at 62.8°F, flow at 899 CFS, gage height 2.03 ft, and turbidity 5.9 NTU, indicating clear and stable tailwater conditions.

Weather Setup

Current sky cover is low at 20%, pressure steady at 30.05 inHg with a neutral trend, winds averaging 5.8 mph from the west and low rain chance with 0.02 inches expected.

Recommended Flies

  • Egg Pattern — Recent stocking makes eggs a logical confidence fly.
  • Squirmy Worm — A simple stocked-trout attractor and good dirty-water option.
  • Duracell — A strong attractor nymph when fish will move a little but are not fully aggressive.

What This Means

Trout will favor oxygenated runs, riffles, and structure, feeding comfortably while avoiding high light. Use clean drifts with small natural patterns and stocked trout attractors like egg patterns, squirmy worms, and Duracell nymphs to target recently stocked fish.
