The Toccoa River offers solid trout fishing over the next 48 hours with generally mild winds, warm air temperatures ranging from low 70s to mid-90s, and variable cloud cover. Recent stocking on June 25th boosts odds for success, especially using stocked-specific patterns. Rain chances remain low to moderate, mostly in the evenings, so plan accordingly.

What This Means

With recent stocking, trout numbers are strong, enhancing catch opportunities especially when using Egg Pattern and stocked-focused flies. Morning hours on both days are prime for active feeding with cooler temps and humidity. Afternoons warm up quickly, favoring nymph and attractor tactics like Hare’s Ear and Duracell. Evening showers on day 1 may push trout to feed aggressively before rain arrives, making streamer or midge patterns effective. Overall, anglers targeting the Toccoa River tailwater can expect good dry and subsurface fly fishing with a range of recommended flies tailored to hatch cycles and stocked fish activity.
