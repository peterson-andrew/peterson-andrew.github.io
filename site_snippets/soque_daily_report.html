Soque River 48-Hour Fishing Outlook

Quick Read

Rating: Fair (5.0/10).

Fishing conditions on the Soque River are fair with recent stocking enhancing trout activity. Bright skies and high pressure create a technical but fishable environment.

Best Window

Best window: day1 evening.

The evening of day 1 is best due to moderate sky cover and stable conditions, aligning with trout favoring comfortable feeding lanes close to cover under bright, high-pressure conditions.

Current River Conditions

Soque River current flow is 65.1 cfs with a gage height of 3.56 ft; water temperature and turbidity data are unavailable.

Weather Setup

Current weather features low average sky cover (23.2%), high pressure at 30.13 inHg, calm ESE winds averaging 2.2 mph, and low rain probability.

Recommended Flies

  • Egg Pattern — Recent stocking makes eggs a logical confidence fly.
  • Squirmy Worm — A simple stocked-trout attractor and good dirty-water option.
  • Duracell — A strong attractor nymph when fish will move a little but are not fully aggressive.
  • Zebra Midge — Small and natural for clear, pressured, technical conditions.
  • Pheasant Tail — A reliable natural nymph when trout are feeding but cautious.

What This Means

Trout will position in shade, deeper runs, undercut banks, and near structure. Use smaller natural flies and careful drifts with less aggressive presentations. Recommended flies include egg pattern, squirmy worm, and duracell for stocked trout, plus zebra midge and pheasant tail for clear, technical water. Exercise caution as bright, high pressure conditions make trout cover-oriented and less forgiving.
