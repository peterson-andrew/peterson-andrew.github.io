Soque River 48-Hour Fishing Outlook

Quick Read

Fishing on the Soque River over the next 48 hours is rated as Fair. Bright conditions paired with high pressure will challenge anglers to adopt careful, technical presentations. Recent stocking activity boosts your chances when using stocked trout patterns.

Best Windows

The prime fishing window is the evening of Day 1, when light clouds and moderate humidity create favorable conditions. Expect trout to hold tight to cover during this period, requiring stealthy approaches.

Conditions Breakdown

Current USGS data shows stable flow at 65.1 cfs with a gage height of 3.56 feet, indicating typical freestone river conditions. Water temperature is unavailable.

Day 1 evening features partly cloudy skies (avg 35% coverage), moderate humidity (~49%), and low but present rain chances (~17%). Winds remain light from the east. On Day 2, mornings and afternoons bring clearer skies with very low rain chances, cooler temperatures in the morning, and slightly warmer afternoons. Evening conditions on Day 2 are warmer with moderate winds, but rain chances remain low.

Recommended Flies

  • Egg Pattern – Ideal given recent stocking, offers confidence for stocked trout.
  • Squirmy Worm – Reliable stocked-trout attractor, also effective if water clarity drops.
  • Duracell – Strong attractor for trout willing to move but not aggressively feeding.
  • Zebra Midge – Small and natural, perfect for pressured trout in clear water.
  • Pheasant Tail – Classic natural nymph, suited for cautious feeding trout.

What This Means

Trout on the Soque River will likely be positioned near shade, deeper runs, undercut banks, and structure to avoid bright, high-pressure conditions. Expect the fish to be cautious and cover-oriented. Employ smaller, natural fly patterns and focus on careful drifts with less aggressive presentations. The recent stocking event adds extra fishing opportunity with stocked-trout flies, improving your odds especially in the evening of day 1.
