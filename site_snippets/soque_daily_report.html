Soque River 48-Hour Fishing Outlook

Quick Read

Rating: Fair (5.0/10).

Trout fishing on the Soque River is fair with technical conditions due to high pressure and low cloud cover. Recent stocking supports using eggs, worms, and attractor nymphs for best results.

Best Window

Best window: day1 morning.

Day 1 morning is the best window because stable high pressure and low wind provide ideal, though technical, fishing conditions with trout favoring shade and deeper runs.

Current River Conditions

USGS reports a flow of 65.1 cfs and gage height of 3.56 ft; water temperature and turbidity data are unavailable.

Weather Setup

Sky cover averages around 20%, pressure is high at 30.11 inHg with stable trend unknown, wind is light from the east at 2.2 mph, and rain probability is low at 14%.

Recommended Flies

  • Egg Pattern — Recent stocking makes eggs a logical confidence fly.
  • Squirmy Worm — A simple stocked-trout attractor and good dirty-water option.
  • Duracell — A strong attractor nymph when fish will move a little but are not fully aggressive.
  • Zebra Midge — Small and natural for clear, pressured, technical conditions.
  • Pheasant Tail — A reliable natural nymph when trout are feeding but cautious.

What This Means

Trout will hold near shade, undercut banks, and deeper runs to stay comfortable in bright, high-pressure conditions. Use smaller natural flies and careful drifts with less aggressive presentations, and prioritize stocked-trout attractors like egg pattern, squirmy worm, and duracell. Exercise caution as fish may be cover-oriented and selective.
